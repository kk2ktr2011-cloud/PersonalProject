Intro

This page will contain information that I think is important to being able to field well. In Cricket, fielding is about getting the ball before it reaches the boundary. This includes things as simple as catching and pushing a ball away from the boundary to decrease its momentum.

Fielding

One of the many proverbs of Cricket I have heard is "Catches win Matches". True enough, many matches have been decided by a single drop or catch (T20 World Cup Final 2024). Therefore, it is important that your fielding is as good as your batting and bowling. I would suggest practicing catching the most, as I see it as the most important part of fielding. For some practice, you could throw a ball as high as you can in the air and then try to to see if you can catch it. Obviously, be aware of the ground you are standing in, as a misstep can cause you to fall. This can teach you to be aware of your surroundings if you ever take a catch in a game.

Catching Tips

  • Use two hands whenever possible because you are more likely to not drop the ball
  • Never take your eyes off the ball, but be aware of your surroundings because you could trip or bash into someone
  • Guess where the ball is going to land and run towards it so that you can take time to catch it
  • Take your time to make sure you are in the right place if and only if you anticipate that the ball is going to take a long time to go down
  • Train your reflexes for quick catches when you are in the slip positions because those types of catches will require you to be quick
  • Practice catching with gloves if you are a wicket-keeper because that is how they play in a match
  • Yell "Mine!" when going to catch a ball to avoid collisions

Practices You Can Do

  • To practice slip and bowler catching, throw the ball at the ground so that it will bounce off a wall back to you; try to catch
  • To practice outfield catching, throw the ball in to the air high and try to catch it as it falls back down
  • To practice ball tracking, throw the ball high in the air and predict the place that you think it is going to fall in and go catch it
  • Another slip catching practice is to make someone standing a few meters away from you throw a ball, and your job is to catch it
  • To practice fielding the ball from going to the boundary, make someone roll the ball really quickly and try to stop it from going past you; similar to Soccer

Ground Fielding

  • Stay low with your knees bent and your weight on the balls of your feet so you can react quickly
  • Keep your eyes on the ball from the moment it is hit until it is safely in your hands
  • Move your feet to get your body behind the ball instead of reaching for it
  • Collect the ball with two hands whenever possible to reduce the chance of a fumble
  • If the ball is moving quickly, get one knee behind it as a backup in case it slips through your hands
  • Approach the ball under control. Running too fast can make it harder to pick up cleanly
  • After collecting the ball, transfer it quickly into your throwing hand so you can return it without delay
  • Aim your throw at the wicketkeeper's gloves or the bowler's end instead of simply throwing as hard as possible
  • Communicate with teammates by calling for the ball or letting others know you are fielding it
  • Practice both forehand and backhand pickups so you can field balls on either side of your body

Returning the Ball

  • Throw the ball to the stumps that is closest to you
  • If both stumps are equally closer, throw it to the side where the batsman is shortest of the crease
  • If both are within the crease, just throw it to the Wicket-Keeper
  • Underarm throws are better when you can take your time, as they are more accurate at shorter distances
  • Overarm throws are better when you have less time, as they are faster at a longer distance
